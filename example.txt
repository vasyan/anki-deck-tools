<h3>เธอ (thəə)</h3>

<h3>Pronunciation and Tone:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>เธอ</strong> = mid tone, one syllable</li>
<li><strong>IPA:</strong> [thəə]</li>
<li><strong>Romanization:</strong> thəə</li>
</ul>

<h3>Basic Meaning:</h3>
<p>Informal pronoun meaning "you" (singular), used with close friends, family, or people of similar age/status.</p>

<h3>Real-Life Examples:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>เธอไปไหนมา</strong> [thəə pai nǎi maa] = "Where did you go?"<br>
<em>Situation: Asking your spouse when they come home from work</em></li>

<li><strong>เธอจะกินข้าวไหม</strong> [thəə jà gin kâao mǎi] = "Are you going to eat?"<br>
<em>Situation: Asking a colleague during lunch break</em></li>

<li><strong>เธอชอบแมวด้วยเหรอ</strong> [thəə chɔ̂ɔp mɛɛo dûai rə̌ə] = "You like cats too?"<br>
<em>Situation: Discovering common interests with a neighbor</em></li>
</ul>

<h3>Usage Notes:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formality Level:</strong> Casual/Informal</li>
<li><strong>When to Use:</strong> With friends, family, close colleagues of similar age</li>
<li><strong>When NOT to Use:</strong> With strangers, older people, formal business situations</li>
<li><strong>Cultural Note:</strong> Can sound too familiar if used with wrong person</li>
</ul>

<h3>Related Words:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>คุณ</strong> [kun] = you (polite, formal)</li>
<li><strong>ท่าน</strong> [tâan] = you (very respectful, for elders)</li>
<li><strong>เรา</strong> [rao] = we/us (can also mean "you" in casual speech)</li>
</ul>

<!-- Example 2: Common Verb -->
<h3>กิน (gin)</h3>

<h3>Pronunciation and Tone:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>กิน</strong> = mid tone, one syllable</li>
<li><strong>IPA:</strong> [gin]</li>
<li><strong>Romanization:</strong> gin</li>
</ul>

<h3>Basic Meaning:</h3>
<p>To eat, consume food. One of the most essential verbs for daily conversation.</p>

<h3>Real-Life Examples:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>กินข้าวกันไหม</strong> [gin kâao gan mǎi] = "Shall we eat?" (literally "eat rice")<br>
<em>Situation: Inviting colleagues to lunch</em></li>

<li><strong>ลูกกินผักไหม</strong> [lûuk gin pàk mǎi] = "Does your child eat vegetables?"<br>
<em>Situation: Discussing parenting with another parent</em></li>

<li><strong>กินแล้วครับ</strong> [gin lɛ́ɛo kráp] = "I've already eaten" (male speaker)<br>
<em>Situation: Declining food offer from building security guard</em></li>
</ul>

<h3>Usage Notes:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formality Level:</strong> Neutral (used in all contexts)</li>
<li><strong>Cultural Note:</strong> "กินข้าว" (eat rice) means "to have a meal" regardless of what you're eating</li>
<li><strong>Common Pattern:</strong> กิน + food item = eat [that food]</li>
</ul>

<h3>Related Words:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>ทาน</strong> [taan] = to eat (more polite/formal)</li>
<li><strong>รับประทาน</strong> [ráp prà taan] = to eat (very polite/formal)</li>
<li><strong>อิ่ม</strong> [ìm] = full (after eating)</li>
<li><strong>หิว</strong> [hǐo] = hungry</li>
</ul>

<br><br>

<!-- Example 3: Shopping/Money Word -->
<h3>เท่าไหร่ (tâo rài)</h3>

<h3>Pronunciation and Tone:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>เท่า</strong> = falling tone</li>
<li><strong>ไหร่</strong> = falling tone</li>
<li><strong>IPA:</strong> [tâo rài]</li>
<li><strong>Romanization:</strong> tâo rài</li>
</ul>

<h3>Basic Meaning:</h3>
<p>How much? Used to ask about price, quantity, or amount. Essential for shopping and transactions.</p>

<h3>Real-Life Examples:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>นี่เท่าไหร่ครับ</strong> [nîi tâo rài kráp] = "How much is this?"<br>
<em>Situation: Asking price at street market or convenience store</em></li>

<li><strong>ค่าแท็กซี่เท่าไหร่</strong> [kâa tɛ́k sîi tâo rài] = "How much is the taxi fare?"<br>
<em>Situation: Asking taxi driver before getting in</em></li>

<li><strong>เงินเดือนเท่าไหร่</strong> [ngən dɯan tâo rài] = "How much is the salary?"<br>
<em>Situation: Discussing job opportunities with spouse</em></li>
</ul>

<h3>Usage Notes:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formality Level:</strong> Neutral (add ครับ/ค่ะ for politeness)</li>
<li><strong>Common Pattern:</strong> [item/service] + เท่าไหร่ = How much is [item/service]?</li>
<li><strong>Response Expected:</strong> Price in Thai baht or quantity</li>
</ul>

<h3>Related Words:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>ราคา</strong> [raa kaa] = price</li>
<li><strong>เงิน</strong> [ngən] = money</li>
<li><strong>บาท</strong> [bàat] = baht (Thai currency)</li>
<li><strong>แพง</strong> [pɛɛng] = expensive</li>
<li><strong>ถูก</strong> [tùuk] = cheap</li>
</ul>

<br><br>

<!-- Example 4: Transportation Word -->
<h3>ไป (pai)</h3>

<h3>Pronunciation and Tone:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>ไป</strong> = mid tone, one syllable</li>
<li><strong>IPA:</strong> [pai]</li>
<li><strong>Romanization:</strong> pai</li>
</ul>

<h3>Basic Meaning:</h3>
<p>To go, to move away from current location. Essential verb for directions and transportation.</p>

<h3>Real-Life Examples:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>ไปสีลมครับ</strong> [pai sǐi lom kráp] = "To Silom please"<br>
<em>Situation: Telling taxi driver your destination</em></li>

<li><strong>เธอไปทำงานแล้วเหรอ</strong> [thəə pai tam ngaan lɛ́ɛo rə̌ə] = "Have you gone to work already?"<br>
<em>Situation: Texting spouse in the morning</em></li>

<li><strong>ไปซื้อของกันไหม</strong> [pai sɯ́ɯ kɔ̌ɔng gan mǎi] = "Shall we go shopping?"<br>
<em>Situation: Weekend plans with family</em></li>
</ul>

<h3>Usage Notes:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Formality Level:</strong> Neutral (used in all contexts)</li>
<li><strong>Direction:</strong> Always indicates movement away from speaker's current location</li>
<li><strong>Common Pattern:</strong> ไป + destination = go to [place]</li>
</ul>

<h3>Related Words:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>มา</strong> [maa] = to come (movement toward speaker)</li>
<li><strong>กลับ</strong> [glàp] = to return, go back</li>
<li><strong>เดิน</strong> [dəən] = to walk</li>
<li><strong>ขับ</strong> [kàp] = to drive</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Additional Insight:</strong><br>
The verb ไป is one of the most frequently used words in Thai. It's essential for giving directions, making plans, and describing daily activities.</p>
